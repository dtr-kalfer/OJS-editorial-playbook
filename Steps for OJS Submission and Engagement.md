# Steps for OJS Submission and Engagement
A Comprehensive Guide for Authors, Reviewers, and Editors

## ✍️ PHASE 1: THE AUTHOR (Initial Submission)

### Step 1: Login & Authentication
* **Instruction:** Access the journal's URL and log in using your registered account.
* **💡 Pro-Tip:** If you have multiple roles (e.g., Author and Reviewer), ensure your profile is configured for "Author" access.
* **⚠️ Common Mistake:** Creating a brand new account every time you submit. Use your existing credentials to keep your publication history in one place.

### Step 2: Initialize New Submission
* **Instruction:** From your dashboard, click the **“New Submission”** button to start the five-step OJS wizard.

### Step 3: Submission Requirements & Privacy
* **Instruction:** Confirm all checkboxes regarding formatting, originality, and copyright.
* **🚫 What NOT to do:** Don’t just "click-spam" through these. These are legal and ethical confirmations. Ensure your manuscript actually meets the journal's specific style guide (e.g., APA, MLA) before checking the box.

### Step 4: Upload Manuscript (The "Blind" Upload)
* **Instruction:** Upload your file (usually `.docx` or `.pdf`).
* **📝 Crucial Note:** Since this is a **double-blind review**, you **must** remove all identifying information. 
    * **Check the Document Properties:** In Word, go to `File > Info > Check for Issues > Inspect Document` to remove personal metadata.
    * **Check the Text:** Ensure "Dr. Smith’s previous study (2023)" isn't written in a way that identifies you as Dr. Smith.
* **⚠️ Common Mistake:** Naming the file `John_Doe_Final_Draft.docx`. Rename it to something neutral like `Manuscript_Submission_OJS.docx`.

### Step 5: Enter Metadata (The "SEO" of Research)
* **Instruction:** Input the Title, Abstract, Keywords, and **all** Co-authors.
* **💡 Pro-Tip:** Ensure the abstract you paste here matches the one inside your document perfectly. 
* **⚠️ Common Mistake:** Forgetting to add co-authors. Adding them *after* the paper is accepted is often a massive administrative headache.

### Step 6: Confirmation & Finish
* **Instruction:** Review the submission summary and click **"Finish Submission."**
* **Expected Result:** You should see a confirmation screen and receive an automated email. If you don't get the email, check your spam folder immediately.

---

## ⚖️ PHASE 2: THE EDITOR (Initial Handling)

### Step 7: Receive & Screen Submission
* **Instruction:** Check `Submissions → Unassigned`.
* **💡 Pro-Tip:** This is the "Desk Reject" phase. If the paper is completely off-topic or poorly formatted, save everyone time and reject it now.

### Step 8: Assign Section Editor
* **Instruction:** Assign yourself or a specialized editor to manage the workflow.
* **🚫 What NOT to do:** Don't leave submissions in "Unassigned" for weeks. Authors are nervous; keep the momentum going!

### Step 9: Initiation of Peer Review
* **Instruction:** Move the submission to the **Review Stage**, select at least two reviewers, and send the formal invitation.
* **⚠️ Common Mistake:** Forgetting to set a **Due Date**. Without a deadline, the review might languish in a reviewer’s inbox forever.

---

## 👓 PHASE 3: THE REVIEWER (The Quality Filter)

### Step 10: Accept or Decline the Invite
* **Instruction:** Check your email or dashboard. Click the link to **Accept** or **Decline**.
* **💡 Pro-Tip:** If you are too busy or have a conflict of interest, click "Decline" immediately so the editor can find a replacement. "Ghosting" an editor is the cardinal sin of academia.

### Step 11: Download & Evaluate
* **Instruction:** Download the anonymized manuscript and review it against the journal's standards.
* **🚫 What NOT to do:** Do not attempt to "de-anonymize" the author or search for the paper title online to find out who wrote it.

### Step 12: Submit Recommendation
* **Instruction:** Fill out the evaluation form and select a recommendation (Accept, Revisions Required, or Reject).
* **📝 Crucial Note:** Be specific in your "Comments for Author." Avoid vague phrases like "The writing is bad." Instead, use "The methodology section lacks detail on sample size."
* **⚠️ Common Mistake:** Uploading an annotated PDF that still has your name in the "Comments" or "Track Changes" bubbles. Check your Word/Adobe "User Identity" settings first!

---

## 📢 PHASE 4: THE EDITOR (The Final Word)

### Step 13: Final Editorial Decision
* **Instruction:** Review the feedback from all reviewers and make a final call: **Accept**, **Revise** (Minor/Major), or **Reject**.
* **💡 Pro-Tip:** If two reviewers disagree (one says "Accept," one says "Reject"), you may need to assign a third "tie-breaker" reviewer.

### Step 14: Notify the Author
* **Instruction:** Use the built-in OJS email templates to send the decision. 
* **🚫 What NOT to do:** Do not send the decision without including the reviewer comments. Authors need that feedback to improve, even if they are being rejected.

---

> **Final Note:** OJS is a "linear" system. Skipping a step or failing to "Record a Decision" can sometimes lock the submission in a previous stage, making it invisible to the next person in the chain. Always ensure you click the final "Record" or "Submit" buttons!
