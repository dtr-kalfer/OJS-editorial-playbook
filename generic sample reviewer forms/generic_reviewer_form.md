## Section 1: Evaluation Criteria (Rate each from 1–5)

**Scale:**  
- Very Poor  
- Poor  
- Acceptable  
- Good  
- Very Good  

---

### Originality / Contribution to Knowledge *
Does the manuscript offer new insights, perspectives, or findings? Evaluate whether it adds meaningful value to existing knowledge in the field.

---

### Relevance to Journal Scope *
Assess whether the topic and approach align with the journal’s aims, scope, and intended audience.

---

### Clarity of Research Objectives / Questions *
Are the research objectives, questions, or hypotheses clearly stated and well-defined?

---

### Appropriateness of Methodology *
Evaluate whether the chosen research design, methods, and procedures are suitable for addressing the research questions.

---

### Quality of Data / Evidence (or argumentation for theoretical papers) *
Assess the adequacy, reliability, and relevance of the data or evidence presented. For theoretical work, evaluate the strength and coherence of the arguments.

---

### Analysis and Interpretation *
Are the data or arguments analyzed appropriately? Are interpretations logical, well-supported, and clearly explained?

---

### Organization and Structure *
Is the manuscript logically organized (e.g., introduction, methods, results, discussion)? Does the flow of ideas make sense?

---

### Clarity of Writing and Language *
Evaluate the clarity, readability, and overall quality of writing, including grammar and coherence.

---

### Use of Literature / References *
Are relevant sources appropriately cited? Does the manuscript engage with current and significant literature in the field?
