# 🧑‍⚖️ How to Assign a Reviewer in OJS

This guide walks editors through assigning a reviewer to a submission in Open Journal Systems (OJS).

---

## 🎯 When to Use This

Assign a reviewer when:

* A new submission is ready for evaluation
* The submission is in the **Review Stage**
* An editor has been assigned to handle the manuscript

---

## 🧭 Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Open the Submission

* Go to your **Dashboard**
* Navigate to **Submissions**
* Click on the target manuscript

---

### 2. Go to the Review Stage

* Inside the submission page, click the **“Review”** tab
* This is where peer review is managed

---

### 3. Add a Reviewer

* Click **“Add Reviewer”**

You will see:

* A list of available reviewers
* Search and filter options

---

### 4. Select a Reviewer

Choose a reviewer based on:

* Expertise / subject alignment
* Availability
* Past performance (if known)

Click **“Select”** next to the reviewer’s name.

---

### 5. Set Review Details

You may configure:

* **Due date** (recommended: 1–2 weeks or based on your policy)
* **Review type** (e.g., double-blind, open review)
* **Custom message** (optional but encouraged)

---

### 6. Send the Review Request

* Click **“Send”** to notify the reviewer

The reviewer will receive an email invitation.

---

## 🔄 What Happens Next?

After sending:

* Status becomes **“Review Requested”**
* Reviewer can **Accept** or **Decline**
* If accepted → review process begins

---

## ⚠️ Common Tips

* ✔ Assign at least **2 reviewers** for better evaluation (recommended)
* ✔ Always set a **clear deadline**
* ✔ Follow up if reviewer does not respond
* ✔ Avoid assigning reviewers with conflicts of interest

---

## 🧠 Good Practice

A strong review process improves:

* Article quality
* Journal credibility
* Author satisfaction

---

## ❗ Troubleshooting

### Reviewer not responding?

* Send a reminder
* Or assign a new reviewer

### No available reviewers?

* Add a new reviewer in **Users & Roles → Reviewers**

---

## 📌 Summary

Assigning reviewers is a key editorial responsibility.
Choosing the right reviewer and setting clear expectations helps ensure a smooth and credible review process.

---

*Tip: Build a reliable reviewer pool early—it saves a lot of time later.*
